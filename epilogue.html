HOW DID WE GET HERE?
Debating Transhumanism and the Enhancement
of Nature, the Human and Human Nature

Abstract

In this age of advancing technology we are becoming increasingly impatient with the body’s nature has given us with our frailties and failing sight our broken limbs, the crippling limitations are lines and and age. While our mind soar, our bodies seem to frozen in time, what if we can build a better body? Use technology to fix this human form and then improve upon it. Make it stronger, faster, smarter and create the human of the future. But what for? Transhumanists, or those who are loyal to the ideals of the improvement of the human species threw enhancement as a moral obligation, believe they are the suppliers of the torch that will take us to an enlightened future. Transhumanism wants to dominate and modify the human nature, and take control of its human evolution to turn it into a post-human society, making the course of nature’s future a malleable property. On the present moment the humankind has the power to modify its environment and it’s own nature, this that before were just mere wishes or pure imagination; and as Tranhumanist believe the expression of a better (enhanced) humankind. This new posibilites of intervention had brought to the light the long debate of the relationship of nature, human and human nature. The human being in the present has the power to modify his environment and his own nature in ways that, in a not so distant past, were only pure fantasy. These new possibilities of intervention have brought back to the light the old debate about human nature and raise new questions about the consequences of this possible intervention. Throughout this work, I will question the implementation of transhumanist arguments and their philosophical ethics, counterposing them with the notion of nature. The eager of humankind to have control of nature. Because their ideology is based on only new technological developments has to be discussed and analysed in depth, taking into account both the benefits and possible harm of their implication, as well as the consequences on human nature itself.
